# Demo Video — Recording Script (target: 3–4 minutes)

I can't record screen video myself, but the app is built so this script maps
directly onto it, scene by scene. Use any screen recorder (OBS, Loom,
Windows Game Bar, QuickTime) at 1080p, and just narrate while you click
through in this order.

## Scene 1 — Hook (0:00–0:20)
Open on the **Command Center**. Say:
> "India imports 88% of its crude oil, and 42% of that transits the Strait
> of Hormuz. Strait Watch turns that structural risk into a continuously
> monitored, decision-ready system."
Point at the pulse strip ticking live in the header.

## Scene 2 — Problem framing (0:20–0:45)
Briefly show the four KPI cards on the Command Center: composite risk,
reserve cover, import dependency, active alerts. Say:
> "Traditional supply planning tools assume predictable environments. Strait
> Watch assumes the opposite — it's built around five agents that watch,
> model, decide, and remember."

## Scene 3 — Risk Intelligence (0:45–1:15)
Go to **Risk Intelligence**. Drag the Hormuz "tension" and "incidents"
sliders up and show the corridor score and badge change live. Say:
> "Every corridor is scored from four signal families — geopolitical
> tension, incidents, sanctions, congestion — using an explicit, documented
> weighting, not a black box."

## Scene 4 — Scenario Simulator (1:15–2:00)
Go to **Scenario Simulator**. Select "Strait of Hormuz — Partial Closure",
push severity to ~50%, duration to 30 days. Say:
> "This is the core of the challenge: converting a geopolitical event into
> an economic one." 
Point at the supply gap, refinery run-rate hit, retail fuel price impact
in INR, GDP drag in bps, and the SPR exhaustion countdown. Show the
depletion chart.

## Scene 5 — Procurement Orchestrator (2:00–2:30)
Click "Send this gap to the Procurement Orchestrator". Say:
> "The same gap now drives a multi-criteria ranking of alternate crude
> sources — cost, availability, distance, refinery compatibility, and the
> live corridor risk score — with a greedy allocation plan that closes the
> gap in barrels, not just percentages."

## Scene 6 — Reserve Optimizer (2:30–2:55)
Navigate to **Reserve Optimizer**. Say:
> "In parallel, the reserve agent smooths the drawdown across the whole
> disruption window and calculates exactly which day to trigger emergency
> procurement, factoring in the real lead time — so policymakers get a
> decision point, not just a countdown."

## Scene 7 — Digital Twin (2:55–3:20)
Go to **Digital Twin**. Click the Strait of Hormuz node, then toggle the
reroute overlay. Say:
> "And this is the geospatial layer tying it together — corridors,
> chokepoints, and India's refineries, with a one-click reroute overlay via
> the Cape of Good Hope."

## Scene 8 — Close (3:20–3:40)
Return to **Architecture** page, show the diagram and the judging-criteria
table for two seconds each. Say:
> "Everything here is deterministic and documented so it can be re-tested
> — and the data layer is built to swap mock feeds for live AIS, news, and
> ERP connectors without touching the model logic. Thanks for watching."

## Recording tips
- Resize the browser window to at least 1280×800 before recording.
- Use the "Show reroute overlay" toggle *after* clicking a node once, so
  the info panel already has content on screen.
- Keep each scene to one clear action — don't rapid-fire the sliders.
- Export as MP4, keep it under 4 minutes; judges skim.
